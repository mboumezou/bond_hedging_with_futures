The objective of this work is to build a simple, clear, and market-oriented bond hedging exercise. We start with a long position on a French sovereign bond, and then seek to reduce its interest rate risk using Bund futures contracts.

The reasoning intentionally follows the basic steps of a fixed-income project: retrieving prices, converting clean price to dirty price, calculating the yield to maturity, calculating the duration, deducing the DV01, and finally sizing the futures position. 
The last section discusses the limitations of the hedge, notably the spread risk between France and Germany, as well as the intuition behind convexity.
